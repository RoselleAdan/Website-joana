About Us

Alma Mater Song

Welcome to Rosales Senior High School! We are committed to providing quality education and fostering a positive learning environment for our students. Our dedicated faculty and staff are here to support and guide our students in their academic journey. With a wide range of programs and extracurricular activities, we strive to develop well-rounded individuals who are prepared for success in college and beyond. Come and join our school community and experience the Rosales difference!

Rosales National High School was founded in 1945 through the initiative of public school officials and prominent and civic-spirited of the town, namely; the late Mayor Felix Coloma, Dona Petra Callanta, Don Francisco delos Reyes, Don Isidro Casanova, Don Conrado Estrella Sr. In September 1945, the school then known as Rosales Junior High School has under each education wing the first year students in 1941 that were overtaken by the outbreak of Pacific war in World War II on December 8, 1941.

STEM

STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. It is a specialized educational track for senior high school students in the Philippines. The STEM strand prepares students for careers in these fields.

The STEM strand is an inquiry and research-based program. It exposes students to complex mathematical and science theories and concepts that will serve as a foundation for their college courses. The STEM strand is for those looking to start a career in science and technology-related fields such as: Engineering, Architecture, Information Technology, Natural Sciences, Nursing, Mathematics, Statistics.

One of the benefits of STEM strand graduates is the diversity in job opportunities. Careers in STEM industries are always in-demand.

HUMSS

HUMSS stands for Humanities and Social Sciences. It's an academic strand that studies human behavior and society's changes. It also involves analyzing human interactions in the fields of literature, arts, culture, and politics.

The HUMSS strand is designed for senior high school students who want to take up journalism, communication arts, liberal arts, education, and other social-science related courses in college. HUMSS students are expected to develop the following skills: A deeper understanding of culture, politics, literature, arts, and society

How to appreciate the complexity of these factors during pressing issues concerning them Critical thinking and understanding activities Some jobs available for graduating HUMSS students include: Writers, Lawyers, Teachers, Reporters, Therapists, Journalists, Counsellors, College Professors.

ABM

ABM stands for Accountancy, Business, and Management. It's a senior high school strand for students interested in business, finance, entrepreneurship, and accounting. The ABM strand prepares students for the corporate world by providing them with the skills and knowledge they need to work in business.

The ABM strand curriculum provides a foundation in the principles of business and management, as well as financial and accounting concepts. It also introduces students to the fundamentals of business, accounting, marketing, and economics. The ABM strand covers 15 core subjects, 7 applied or contextualized subjects, and 9 specialized subjects.

What jobs are in ABM?

The ABM strand could lead to fruitful careers in management and accounting, business administration, and entrepreneurship. These professions include sales manager, human resources manager, marketing director, project officer, bookkeeper, accounting clerk, internal auditor, and business owner, among others.

GAS

GAS stands for General Academic Strand. It is a strand in Senior High School that prepares students for university life. GAS is one of four strands in the Academic Track for Senior High School.

GAS is a preparatory course for students to take baccalaureate courses in humanities, Social Sciences, Entrepreneurship, and Business Management after grade 12. It covers 15 core subjects, 7 applied or contextualized subjects, and 9 specialized subjects. Some subjects in the GAS strand include: Oral Communication in Context Reading and Writing Skills 21st Century Literature from the Philippines and the World Understanding Culture, Society and Politics Earth and Life Science Physical Science Introduction to the Philosophy of the Human Person.

GAS is designed for students who are not yet sure of what course or degree they want to take in college. It covers many subjects from the other academic strands, such as humanities, organization and management, and social sciences.

Students in the GAS strand can expect to gain the following skills:

Hone creative writing skills
Learn about Organization Management

Learn about Empowerment Technologies

Gain knowledge of Disaster

Readiness and Risk Reduction

Develop communication skills

TVL

TVL stands for Technical Vocational Livelihood. It's a strand in the senior high school curriculum in the Philippines that aims to give students technical and vocational skills for their future career or livelihood.

The TVL strand provides a curriculum that combines core courses and specialized hands-on courses that meet the competency-based assessment of TESDA.

TVL graduates can get jobs as: Call center agents, Web designers, Data encoders, Radio technicians, Graphic designers, Junior programmers.

There are (3) specialized subject under the Technical-Vocational Livelihood (TVL) career track for senior high school students.

1.Home Economics (HE)

2.Information and Communications Technology (ICT)

3.Shielded Metal Arc Welding (SMAW)

Deped Vission/Mission

Vission

We dream of Filipinos who passionately love their country and whose values and competencies enable them to realize their full potential and contribute meaningfully to building the nation. as a learner-centered public institution, the Department of Education continuously improves itself to better serve its stakeholders.

Mission

To protect and promote the right of every Filipino to quality, equitable, culture-based, and complete basic education where: Students learn in a child-friendly, gender-sensitive, safe, and motivating environment. Teachers facilitate learning and constantly nurture every learner. Administrators and staffs, as stewards of the institution, ensure an enabling supportive environment for effective learning to happen. Family, community, and other stakeholders are effectively engaged and share responsibility for developing life-long learners.
